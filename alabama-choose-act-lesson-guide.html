01 — About This Program
→ What is the Alabama CHOOSE Act, and why should I apply? +

The CHOOSE Act (Creating Hope and Opportunity for Our Students' Education Act) is Alabama's ESA program, administered by the Alabama Department of Revenue (ALDOR). It provides refundable income tax credits — called education savings accounts — that eligible K5–12 families can use to pay for approved educational expenses at approved providers. You won't see the word "ESA" on the site itself, but that's what it is.

All payments flow through ClassWallet, ALDOR's platform partner. As an approved Education Service Provider (ESP), families can find you in ClassWallet and pay for your classes directly from their account.

02 — Before You Start
→ What do I need to have ready before applying? +

Have these on hand before you open the application — it'll save you from stopping midway.

1
Your legal business name (e.g. Megapixels, LLC) and your preferred display name (e.g. Megapixels School of the Arts)
2
Your business address, phone, and website
3
Your "Is Your Student Creative?" vendor blurb from the Organize Now lesson — this goes in the description field
4
If applying as a tutor: proof of a bachelor's degree, state certification, or accreditation — must be uploaded at time of application
5
Your ClassWallet account — if you've done another ESA that uses ClassWallet, you already have one
⚠ This application assumes you've already set up ClassWallet. If you haven't, watch the ClassWallet setup video first — it's covered in the earlier ESA lessons.
03 — Provider Type & Categories
→ Which service provider categories should I select? +

You can — and should — select more than one category if they apply. The more categories you're listed under, the more often families will find you when browsing or filtering the directory. Here's how to think through each one:

Online Education Classes / Courses
✓ Select this. This is the core category for online class providers.
Private Tutoring
✓ Select this if you offer any one-on-one instruction, including online. Important: selecting this requires uploading proof of a bachelor's degree, state certification, or accreditation at the time of application. Art is explicitly listed as an approved tutoring subject.
Curriculum
Consider this if you offer pre-recorded classes. Technically this category is for providers who only sell curriculum/supplemental materials, but it's worth selecting if you have pre-recorded content. It may get flagged — but it's worth trying for the added visibility.
Private School
Do not select unless you are registered as an accredited K–12 private school in Alabama.
Educational Therapies
Only select if you hold a valid license in the therapy you provide. Not applicable for most art educators.
The directory is searchable and filterable by category. Showing up under both Online Education Classes and Private Tutoring means more families will find you. Don't leave categories on the table if they genuinely apply.
⚠ If a category gets kicked back during review, ALDOR will let you know. It's better to try and get corrected than to under-select and miss families.
04 — Tutor Credential Requirement
→ What do I need to prove if I'm applying as a tutor? +

This is one of the most important fields in the Alabama application. If you select Private Tutoring as a category, you are required by state rules to submit documentation proving one of the following — you only need one, not all:

— A bachelor's degree (any field)
— State certification (e.g. a teaching license)
— Proof of accreditation

This documentation must be uploaded directly in the application at the time you submit. If you're uploading a bachelor's degree, the expiration date field can be filled in as five years from today's date — that's the convention for credentials that don't technically expire.

⚠ A background check is also required for tutors. Both the credential documentation and background check must be submitted at the time of application — not after the fact.

05 — Business Type & Names
→ What do I enter for business type, company name, and display name? +
Business Type
Select LLC if that's your structure. If you're an S Corp, your tax advisor may confirm you're still an LLC at the entity level — select whatever matches your actual registration.
Business / Company Name
Your legal LLC name exactly as registered (e.g. Megapixels, LLC). This is the name that will appear on tax documents and payments.
Display Name
The name families will see in the ClassWallet directory. This is where you can use your DBA or branded name (e.g. Megapixels School of the Arts).
Unlike some other ESAs, Alabama's application has a separate display name field — so you don't have to fight to use your brand name. Put your legal LLC in the company name field and your recognizable name in the display name field.
06 — ClassWallet Marketplace
→ What is the ClassWallet Marketplace, and do I need it? +

The application asks whether you're a Marketplace Vendor. Here's what that means and how to answer it.

Direct Pay Vendor
Families find you in ClassWallet and pay you directly from their ESA. This is the default, requires no e-commerce platform, and is how most providers operate. Select No for Marketplace Vendor if this is you.
Marketplace Vendor
You have your own e-commerce storefront that integrates with ClassWallet, giving you additional visibility in the Marketplace. Requires an e-commerce platform — the most affordable option we've found is around $24/month. Optional for Alabama; required for Indiana.
If you're just getting started with this ESA, select No for Marketplace Vendor. You can always add it later, especially if you join Indiana — at which point it makes sense to set it up across all your ESAs at once.
⚠ The Marketplace increases your visibility but costs money to maintain. Get accepted first, evaluate your student volume, then decide if the monthly cost makes sense for your profit margins.
07 — Product Eligibility
→ Do any of my products fall outside permitted categories? +

This field asks whether any of your products or services fall outside the CHOOSE Act's approved expense categories. For most art educators, the answer is No — art is explicitly listed across multiple approved categories.

Approved categories that apply to online art instruction include:

— Tuition and fees for an approved nonpublic K–12 online learning program (Art is listed)
— Private Tutoring (Art is listed)
— Educational software and applications (Art is listed)
— Instructional Materials (paintbrushes, paints, sketch pads, clay, colored pencils, etc. are all explicitly approved)

If you're unsure whether a specific offering qualifies, check the current ESP Guide's approved expenses list before answering. When in doubt, review the Parent Guide — it lists what families are and aren't allowed to spend funds on.

⚠ ALDOR reviews all transactions and reserves the right to reject invoices that don't fall within approved categories. When you invoice, select the expense category that most accurately matches the service rendered.
08 — Grades & Subjects
→ Which grades and subjects should I select? +

Select all grade levels you actually teach. The CHOOSE Act covers K5–12 — if you teach across that range, select all of them. Narrowing unnecessarily limits the families who can find and use you.

For subjects, select everything that genuinely applies to your classes. For art educators:

Art is explicitly listed as an approved subject under both Private Tutoring and Online Learning Programs. You can also select English Language Arts if your classes include written components, or other elective subjects where applicable.
⚠ Only select subjects you can actually deliver on — ALDOR reviews invoices for compliance with what you've listed in your application.
09 — After You Submit
→ What happens after I submit, and how do I get paid? +

Here's exactly what to expect after you hit submit:

1
ALDOR will notify you of their decision within 14 days. It may be faster.
2
You'll receive a ClassWallet Welcome Email with instructions to complete your integration — connect your bank account and set up your profile.
3
You'll also receive an email that your directory listing is live. When that email arrives, watch the profile activation video before doing anything — most providers don't complete this step correctly, and it directly affects whether families find you.
4
Once set up, families pay you using the "Pay Vendor" feature in ClassWallet. They submit payment, and funds settle to your bank account within 2–10 business days after approval.
Invoicing is required for every payment. Each invoice must include: your provider name and address, the student's name, the parent's name, date of invoice, date(s) of service, type of service, and total amount due. Handwritten invoices will not be approved.
⚠ Send yourself a copy of your responses when you submit — there's an option to do this at the end of the form. Do it. You'll want a record of exactly what you submitted.
10 — Your Directory Profile
→ How does the ClassWallet directory work, and why does my profile matter? +

Once approved, your listing appears in the ClassWallet provider directory — which is how families find and choose you. The directory is searchable and filterable by category, which is exactly why selecting multiple relevant categories in your application matters.

Most approved providers never complete their profile. That means a filled-out profile with a real description and accurate categories instantly makes you stand out. Families can see your company name, website, and description — give them something worth reading.

Through your ClassWallet account, you can update your profile at any time. Your description should be clear about what you offer, who it's for, and why it works — the vendor blurb from the Organize Now lesson is written exactly for this.

⚠ When you receive the "your listing is live" email, watch the profile activation video in this course before touching your profile. There are specific steps that, if skipped, will leave your listing incomplete or invisible in certain searches.
