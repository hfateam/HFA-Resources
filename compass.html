01 — Start Here
→ Compass changed — it's now referral-only +

Compass used to take open applications, but it's changed: right now they only add vendors that a student or family has referred. So it works like the family-request schools — the door opens when a family asks for you, not when you apply cold.

Compass also has a few specific requirements that catch people off guard, so it's worth knowing them before you start.

⚠ The full how-to for getting a family to refer you lives in the family referral lesson — start there. This page covers Compass's own specifics.
02 — How It Works Now
→ The referral path +
1. A Compass family asks Compass to add you as a vendor.
2. Compass opens the door for you to complete the vendor application.
3. You finish your paperwork — mostly from your standard packet.

Since step one is the family, your real move is getting in front of Compass families and encouraging the ask. The family referral lesson walks through exactly how — the newsletter, the Facebook groups, all of it.

03 — What Compass Requires
→ Have these in place before you start +
Before you apply
— You've been in business at least 6 months
— A built-out website where Compass can easily see all your classes, each with a price and a brief description
— Your standard packet, ready to go
⚠ The website piece is the one to sort first if it isn't already — they really do look for an easy-to-browse list of your classes, prices, and descriptions. Get all of that set before you start.
Live vs. pre-recorded — depends where you are
In California
You can offer live classes. Because that means live student contact, a background check is required — sort it before you apply.
Outside California
You can only offer pre-recorded classes — no live classes. The upside: with no live student contact, you don't need a background check at all.
⚠ So if you're out of state, the trade-off is real but simple: classes have to be pre-recorded, and in exchange you skip the whole background-check step.

05 — Keep in Mind
→ The takeaways +

Compass is a referral school now, so you get a family to ask first — the family referral lesson covers how. Before you start, make sure you've been in business 6+ months and your website clearly lists your classes, prices, and descriptions.

And remember the location rule: in California you can teach live (with a background check), while out of state you're pre-recorded only (and skip the background check entirely).
