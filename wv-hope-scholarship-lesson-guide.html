Provider Application Guide
01 — About This Program
→ What is the WV Hope Scholarship, and why should I apply? +

The West Virginia Hope Scholarship is an education savings account (ESA) program that allows qualifying WV families to use state education funds for K–12 educational expenses outside the public school system. As an approved Education Service Provider (ESP), you can receive payment directly through the Hope Scholarship portal when families choose your classes.

You won't see the word "ESA" anywhere on the site — don't let that throw you off. This is it. Look for Education Service Provider or ESP throughout the application.

02 — Part One
→ What is Part 1 of the application? +

Part 1 is the ESP Seal Request Form on the Service Providers page. It's quick — two minutes max. This registers your basic contact information and requests your official Hope Scholarship seal.

1
Fill in your first name, last name, and email address.
2
Enter your legal business name (e.g. Megapixels, LLC — not your DBA or display name).
3
Select whether you want a certificate or window cling mailed to you. If you're fully online, No is fine.
4
Check "I'm not a robot" and click Submit.
⚠ Done! That's all Part 1 requires. Move on to Part 2 immediately after.
03 — Part Two
→ What is Part 2 of the application? +

Part 2 is the New Provider Request Form. Click the link on the Service Providers page. Here's how to fill out each field:

Provider Type
Select Service Provider — not School. Online class providers are non-school service providers under Hope Scholarship program rules, even if a school confirmed this for you.
Educational Products
Select Yes — this applies even for online classes.
Vendor Description
Paste your "Is Your Student Creative?" blurb from the Organize Now lesson here.
Micro School?
Select No. A microschool is a tuition-charging school operating as a full alternative to public/private school. An online class business does not qualify.
Background Checks
Select Yes — but only if your background check is actually complete. See Card 04 for important details on this.
Business Name
Use your legal LLC name exactly. You can email them afterward to ask about displaying a DBA, but they'll likely hold you to the legal name — especially when your W-9 is involved.
Address / City / State / Zip
Fill in your business address as registered.
Website
Your main website URL.
Vendor Portal Address Field
Enter: "Students must appear under the Purchases tab in the Vendor Portal first before they are given access to the class." This field relates to how invoicing flows through the portal — if they follow up, just reply to their email.
Refund Policy
Select Yes. This is required by program rules — Hope Scholarship funds must be refunded for any services not actually delivered to the student. You do not have flexibility to opt out of this.
Timezone / Contact Info
Your timezone, your name (or your team's), your email, and phone number.

Click Submit. The whole form should take about five minutes once you have your information ready.

04 — Background Checks
→ What do I need to know about the background check requirement? +

This is the one field that requires the most care. By selecting Yes on background checks, you are making a legal certification to the Hope Scholarship Board that:

Every employee or person on your team who will have contact with Hope Scholarship students has passed a criminal background check and is not on a federal or state sex offender registry.

This is a requirement under West Virginia law — not just a checkbox. Only select Yes if this is genuinely true for your situation.

⚠ If your COR background check is still in process when you apply: reach out to the Hope Scholarship team directly at help@hopescholarshipwv.com or 681-999-HOPE before submitting, and ask how to handle it. Do not certify something that isn't complete yet. In practice, the odds of getting a WV student before your COR is processed are low — but if it does happen, contact them immediately and ask whether an existing background check (such as through Outschool) can serve temporarily.

05 — Refund Policy
→ What does the Hope Scholarship refund policy actually require? +

The refund field on the application isn't really a choice — it's a program requirement. Here's what the rules say:

Hope Scholarship funds must be refunded to the student's account any time services are not actually delivered. You may only keep funds equal to what you actually provided.

In practice for online class providers:

✓
Student pays for a live class and doesn't show — but the class ran as scheduled. No refund required. Service was rendered.
✓
Student pays for access to a pre-recorded class and you never provide access. Refund required.
✓
Student pays for a semester of services and you only deliver part of it. Prorated refund required.
⚠ Refunds must go directly back to the student's Hope Scholarship account — never directly to the parent. Contact the Hope Scholarship team to process any refund. You have 30 days from the triggering event.
06 — Provider vs. School
→ Why am I a "Service Provider" and not a "School"? +

The Hope Scholarship program has two distinct categories, and the distinction matters for what's required of you.

Participating School
A private or nonpublic school that enrolls Hope Scholarship students for tuition, submits attendance records annually, and follows specific school-level program rules. Microschools and learning pods do not qualify as participating schools.
Non-School Service Provider
Any individual or entity offering qualifying educational services — tutoring, online classes, arts instruction, therapies, etc. This is what we are. Far fewer ongoing requirements, and you invoice per service through the portal.
Online class providers like us are non-school service providers. You do not need to track attendance for the state, submit tuition schedules, or meet school-level compliance requirements. Select Service Provider on your application with confidence.
07 — Getting Paid
→ How does payment actually work once I'm approved? +

All payments go through the Hope Scholarship online portal — there is no invoicing outside the system. Here's the basic flow:

1
A student must appear under the Purchases tab in your Vendor Portal before you can invoice for their class.
2
Once they appear, you can submit an invoice through the portal for the service.
3
Payment is made electronically (ACH) directly to your connected bank account.
You'll need to connect your bank account through the portal before you can receive any funds. See the Connect Your Bank Account guide on the Service Providers page.
⚠ Hope Scholarship funds can never be refunded, rebated, or paid directly to a parent or student. All transactions — including refunds — go through the portal with the help of the Hope Scholarship team.

08 — After You Submit
→ What happens after I submit the application? +

Most providers are approved as long as they offer qualifying educational services — the process is generally straightforward. A few things to know:

All Hope Scholarship communications — including legally required Board notices — go to the primary contact email you listed on your application. Monitor that inbox. If your email changes, update it in the portal immediately.
1
Watch for a confirmation or follow-up email from the Hope Scholarship team.
2
Once approved, connect your bank account through the portal so you can receive payments.
3
Set up your service offerings in the portal. (See the Creating a Service Offering guide on their site.)
4
If anything on your application needs to be corrected, email them — they're easy to work with.
⚠ If any information changes after approval — contact info, bank account, team members — update it in the portal right away. You are responsible for keeping your profile current.
