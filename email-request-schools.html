01 — Start Here
→ These schools don't have a form — you ask first +

For this group, there's no public application sitting on a website waiting for you. Instead, you reach out first — almost always by email, occasionally by phone — and ask whether they're taking new vendors. They reply, and that's when the real process starts.

It feels backwards, but it's actually the easiest kind: one short, friendly email and you're in motion.

⚠ Every client offers something different, so treat the template below as a starting point, not a fill-in-the-blank you copy exactly. Use your own judgment for your niche, and anywhere you see something like this, it's a placeholder — replace it with your own details before you hit send. Questions? Reach out anytime.
02 — The Email
→ Your vendor inquiry email +

Keep it short, warm, and specific. You want to make it easy for them to say yes and easy for them to tell you exactly what's next. Asking your three questions up front means you usually get everything you need in one reply.

Vendor Inquiry Email

Subject: Vendor Inquiry — [YOUR BUSINESS NAME]

Hi [SCHOOL NAME] team,

I'm [YOUR NAME] with [YOUR BUSINESS NAME], and I offer [ONE LINE — WHAT YOU TEACH, e.g. "online art and drawing classes for K–12 homeschool students"]. I'd love to be considered as an approved vendor so your enrolled families can use their funds toward my classes.

When you have a moment, could you let me know:

  • Whether you're currently accepting new vendors
  • What your application or onboarding process looks like
  • Any documents you'll need from me to get started (W-9, certificate of insurance, background check, etc.)

Thank you so much — happy to send over anything you need.

[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR BUSINESS NAME]
[PHONE] · [EMAIL] · [WEBSITE]

⚠ If you don't hear back in a week or so, a short, polite follow-up is completely normal — these inboxes are busy and a gentle nudge often does the trick.
03 — What Comes Next
→ What happens after you hit send +

Every school replies a little differently, but it almost always lands in one of three buckets:

A form
They send you a vendor application to fill out and return.
A portal invite
They add you to an online vendor system where you finish your profile and upload documents.
A document list
They reply with exactly what they need (W-9, COI, background check, etc.) and ask you to send it over.

Whichever it is, the good news is the same: your standard packet already covers most of what they'll ask for. If you've built it (it's the first lesson on this page), you can often respond the same day — which makes a great impression and keeps your momentum.

⚠ Got a reply that asks for something unusual or that you're not sure about? Don't guess — reach out and we'll sort it out together before you send anything off.
