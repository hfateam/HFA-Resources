01 — About This School
→ What is Children First Education Fund, and why does my listing matter here? +

Children First Education Fund (CFEF) is a Utah nonprofit that administers the Carson Smith Opportunity Scholarship (CSOS) — a state-funded scholarship for Utah students with disabilities in grades K–12. As an approved service provider, your listing is how families find and choose you.

The directory is publicly searchable at serviceprovider.cfe-fund.org/directory — families can type keywords to find providers. This means your listing text, title, and categories directly affect whether you show up.
⚠ Note: CFEF recently rebranded their platform. The organization is now called Children First Education Fund (no apostrophe-s), and listings are now referred to as Service Providers, not vendors. Use this language when emailing them.

02 — The Problem
→ Why might my listing not reflect what I submitted on my application? +

CFEF staff create your directory listing on your behalf — often using AI tools fed by your application or website content. You are not notified when this happens, and you won't know it's been created unless you go look.

Common issues to watch for:

— Business name wrong or incomplete (e.g. "Megapixels" instead of "Megapixels School of the Arts")
— Description clearly AI-generated and not in your voice
— Stock photo used instead of your own image
— Wrong or outdated address
— Missing or incorrect keywords / categories
— Age groups not listed or incorrect

Don't assume your listing is accurate just because your application was. Go find yourself in the directory and read every field.

03 — Finding Your Listing
→ How do I find my listing in the directory? +

Go to serviceprovider.cfe-fund.org/directory and type your school or business name in the search bar. Try partial names too — you may show up under a shortened version of your name.

Once you find your listing, check every field carefully:

Business Name
Is it your full, correct name?
Description
Does it sound like you? Is it accurate and keyword-rich?
Title / Tagline
Does it include relevant keywords like "online"? Is it compelling?
Photo
Is it your image, or a stock photo?
Address
Is it current? Online providers may prefer to list as "Online."
Website
Does the link work and go to the right page?
Age Groups
Are all your grade levels listed? (e.g. Kindergarten, Primary, Secondary)
Categories
Do the keywords match what families would actually search?
Disability Categories
If relevant, are you listed under the disabilities you support well?
⚠ Also test your keywords — type in terms like "online art" or "drawing" and see if you appear. If not, make sure those words are actually in your listing text.
04 — Requesting Changes
→ How do I get my listing updated? +

You can't edit your listing directly — you email CFEF and tell them exactly what you want changed. Be specific and organized so they can make all the updates in one pass.

— Correct business name
— Address (or request "Online" if you prefer)

— New title/tagline
— Updated written description (paste it in or attach)
— Age groups you teach
— Categories/keywords you want listed
— Disability categories (if applicable)
— Photo (attach your preferred image — not a stock photo)

Once they've made updates, go back and check that everything actually looks the way you wanted. Don't assume it's correct until you've verified it yourself.

05 — Keywords & Searchability
→ How does search work in this directory, and how do I make sure I show up? +

The CFEF directory search pulls from your full listing text — including your title and description. That means the words you use directly determine whether families find you.

Think about what a parent would actually type. If you teach online art, make sure the word "online" appears in your title or description. If you teach drawing, make sure "drawing" is in there — don't assume it will be inferred from "art."

You can test this yourself: go to the directory and search for keywords you'd expect to show up under. If you don't appear, those words aren't in your listing.

Test "online art" Test "drawing" Test your name Test your niche
⚠ The category/filter system on the directory may have a bug — selecting individual filters may not work. Keyword search in the main bar is the most reliable way families will find you, so prioritize getting your keywords into your text.
06 — Categories
→ What categories should I ask to be listed under? +

It's unclear whether CFEF has a fixed list of categories or allows free-form entries. The safest approach is to email them and ask for the list of available categories — then choose what fits.

If they don't provide a list, just tell them what you offer. For art and creative educators, consider:

— Art
— Drawing
— Photography
— Writing (if you offer it, even occasionally)
— Online (if you teach virtually)

There may be a limit on how many categories you can have — possibly two or three. Prioritize the ones most central to what you teach. If you offer writing but aren't actively marketing it, you can still include it so you're not locked out later.

⚠ We're still confirming whether CFEF has a set category list. Check below this video for any updates — we'll add details here once we hear back.
07 — Disability Categories
→ Should I fill in the Disability Categories section? +

Yes — if your classes work well for students with specific disabilities, this is worth including. The Carson Smith Scholarship is specifically for students with disabilities, so families are actively filtering for providers who can support their child's needs.

In your email to CFEF, list the disability categories that genuinely apply to your teaching. For example: "My classes work well for students with ADHD, dyslexia, and anxiety."

Make sure it's also clear that you welcome students without disabilities — you don't want to accidentally narrow your audience.

You can copy and paste from the category options listed in the directory. Only include what's genuinely true for your classes.
