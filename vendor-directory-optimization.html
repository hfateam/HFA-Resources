How to Optimize Your Vendor Directory Listing

Once you've been accepted to a school, this is how you make sure families can actually find and choose you.

What is a vendor directory? When a school accepts you as a vendor, they typically list you in a directory, essentially a searchable marketplace where homeschool families browse and choose where to spend their education funds.

Think of it like a profile page for your business. Your job is to make yours so clear, compelling, and complete that a parent scrolling quickly stops and says, "Yes, this one."

The good news: most vendors don't put much effort into their listings. That means a little intentionality goes a long, long way.

1

Find Your Listing

After acceptance, locate your listing in the school's vendor directory. Use our school directory to find the specific directory for each school you've been accepted to. Depending on the school, you'll either log in directly to edit your listing, or you'll need to email the school with the changes you'd like made. Both are normal, so just check how each school handles it.

2

Apply the Five Pillars to Every Listing

Whether the directory is public or private, these five things apply to every single listing you have, no exceptions.

✅

Fully Filled Out

Don't leave a single field blank. Parents need complete information to make a buying decision, and the more you fill out, the more often you'll show up in search results within the directory.

🔍

Keyword Rich

Use the words families actually search for: art, photography, drawing, online, and so on. If the words aren't there, you won't show up.

✨

Compelling

Your listing should make a parent feel something. What transformation do you offer their child? What makes you stand out from everyone else? Why are you the perfect fit for their kid specifically? Lead with that, not just your credentials.

💡

Clear

A parent should immediately understand what you offer, who it's for, and how it works. No jargon, no vague language. If it takes effort to understand, they'll move on.

👉

Ends with a Call to Action

Don't just describe what you do. Tell the parent exactly what to do next. Give them a clear, easy next step that feels exciting, not salesy. Examples: Try a free trial class, Grab our free starter guide, Save your spot today, Sign up now (spots fill fast). A listing without a CTA leaves parents with nowhere to go.

3

Public vs. Private Directories

Some school directories are visible to the public, meaning anyone can browse them; others are private, visible only to enrolled families. Here's how your approach shifts:

🔒 Private Directory

Optimize in a vacuum

  • You can't see other vendors' listings
  • Focus entirely on your own profile
  • Apply all four pillars thoroughly
  • That's genuinely all you can do, and it's enough
🌐 Public Directory

Optimize with context

  • Browse listings beyond just your niche
  • Look for ones that genuinely stand out
  • Notice what they're doing that you aren't
  • Get inspired, then apply it to yours
💬 A note on public directories

When you browse other listings, you're not looking to copy anyone. You're looking to learn what's possible. Maybe you didn't know you could add a video. Maybe you didn't know you could include a photo gallery. Maybe another vendor's bio format is just much clearer than yours. That's valuable intel. Use it.

4

Study Strong Examples

What does a great listing actually look like?

Inside HFA, you'll find curated examples of strong vendor listings, real profiles that do this well. When you're optimizing your own, use those as a reference point. You can also find examples yourself: browse any public directory and look for a listing that immediately grabs your attention. Ask yourself what it's doing right. Then bring that energy to yours.

🌿

The goal is simple: when a family finds your listing, they should feel confident, excited, and ready to say yes. A listing that's complete, keyword-rich, compelling, clear, and ends with a strong call to action does exactly that, and it's well within your reach. You've already done the hard part by getting accepted. Now let's make sure families can find you.
