Build Your Flex Classes As You Go

A smarter approach to creating content that students actually want

You don't need a fully built class before your first student. List your flex ideas, let a real student sign up, then build together — one lesson at a time.

The Process
1

List your flex ideas — not finished classes

Put as many flex class ideas as you want on your DLS and website. You don't need the class built. You just need the idea listed and advertised.

2

Wait for a real student to sign up

Once someone enrolls, that's your green light. Now you know there's actual demand — and you have a real student to tailor the content around.

Ask them what they want to learn or what they're struggling with
3

Tell the student: "You'll get access in one week"

This gives you a week to create the first two lessons before opening the class. Two lessons upfront is your buffer — if you get sick, go on vacation, or life happens, your student isn't left waiting.

4

Release one new lesson every week after that

Create one lesson per week going forward. You're building just ahead of your student — not months ahead, not in a vacuum.

5

Don't worry if you start with just one or two students

In most cases, more students come the following year. It's always worth building. The only thing to regret is creating an entire class before anyone signs up — and finding out later it wasn't what students needed.

Why This Works

You can customize the content around the students who are actually in your class — and avoid spending hours creating lessons that nobody ever sees.

The Two-Lesson Buffer

Starting with two lessons means you have built-in breathing room. If something unexpected comes up, your student still has content waiting for them.

What if you're worried about capacity?

You never have to offer a class just because it's on your website or DLS. If you know fall will be too busy, you can put interested students on a waitlist and note that the class is currently full or accepting limited enrollment. You can add language to your listings that students should confirm availability before signing up — something like, "Check with me to make sure this class is currently accepting new students." Only create what you have time for.
